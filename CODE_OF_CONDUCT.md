# Code of conduct

Character UI is an open-source project where contributors should be able to participate without
harassment, intimidation, or exclusion. This code applies to repository discussions, issues, pull
requests, reviews, community spaces, and public interactions made on behalf of the project.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and specific.
- Critique ideas, code, evidence, and outcomes—not people.
- Welcome questions and different levels of experience.
- Give actionable feedback and accept good-faith correction.
- Respect privacy, attribution, licenses, and requests to stop unwanted contact.
- Disclose conflicts of interest that could affect a review or moderation decision.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, stalking, doxxing, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Discrimination or demeaning language based on identity, background, ability, or experience.
- Personal attacks, insults, sustained disruption, or deliberate intimidation.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern or assists an investigation.
- Knowingly submitting secrets, malicious content, plagiarized traits, or misleading provenance.

## Reporting and enforcement

For conduct that can be addressed publicly without exposing a target or sensitive details, open a
concise repository issue requesting maintainer moderation. For a private report, use a private
contact method listed by the repository owner or ask publicly for a private channel without naming
people or describing the incident. Immediate threats and GitHub-platform abuse should be reported
through GitHub's safety and abuse tools.

Maintainers will limit report access to people needed for review, consider the safety and wishes of
affected participants, and avoid conflicts of interest. Reports made in bad faith may themselves be
treated as misconduct; an unproven good-faith report will not.

Proportionate actions may include a private warning, content removal, participation limits, a
temporary suspension, or a permanent ban. Severe threats, harassment, privacy violations, or
malicious submissions may result in immediate removal. Maintainers may also reverse an action when
new evidence shows it was mistaken or disproportionate.

## Maintainer responsibility

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards, applying them consistently, and
recusing themselves when they cannot review a report impartially. They may remove contributions or
comments that violate this code, even when the same content also contains useful technical work.
